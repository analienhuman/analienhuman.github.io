The Purpose of Life and the Role of Evolution

In this post, I’d like to share my thoughts on the purpose of life and evolution. These are not established facts but ideas that spark curiosity and exploration. "Imagination is more important than knowledge because knowledge is limited, while imagination embraces the entire world, driving progress and giving birth to evolution." I am actively working on refining these ideas into a structured theory. The ideas shared in this post build upon the concepts discussed in the previous one. The Nature of Time and Our Place in It

As discussed earlier, the universe, as referred to in all the posts, is a combination of both the Observable Realm and the Dark Realm. Since the Dark Realm formed first, we can say that time began at the moment of its emergence.

I think time in the Dark Realm remains absolute. However, within the Observable Realm, where energy and mass interact, time becomes relative due to the deformation of the surrounding Dark Realm. From the perspective of the Dark Realm, where positive energy forms do not exist, the Observable Universe experiences a lag in time.

Since humans exist within the Observable Realm, we might always be experiencing time with a delay. This makes me think that what we call the present is actually our past, and what we perceive as the future is, in reality, the present from the perspective of the Dark Realm.

A Thought Experiment: Time Lag and Its Consequences

Consider two people with two clocks:

  • One is in the Dark Realm, where time is absolute.
  • The other is in the Observable Realm, where time is lagging.


Let's assume the time lag between these two realms is one hour. If the clock in the Dark Realm shows 3:00 PM, then the clock in the Observable Realm will show 2:00 PM. This means that, from the Dark Realm's perspective, events at 2:00 PM have already happened.

Now, imagine that at 2:30 PM in the Observable Realm, a stone is about to fall on someone's head. The person in the Dark Realm has already seen this event happen. If the individual in the Observable Realm does nothing, the event will unfold as expected. However, because they are lagging behind time, they have the ability to intervene and change the outcome.

This suggests that the Observable Realm has the unique ability to alter its path, making it non-deterministic, whereas the Dark Realm follows a fixed trajectory toward increasing entropy.

The Observable Realm's Role in Maintaining Universal Order

The universe moves along a natural path, where disorder gradually increases over time. Within the Dark Realm exists the Observable Realm, where changes, interactions, and evolving structures introduce order and slow down this inevitable collapse.

To sustain stability, everything within the Observable Realm must follow a certain order. Consider the solar system, it exists in a structured state, and for it to remain so, its individual components, including Earth, must also stay in harmony. Extending this further, if Earth must remain stable, then living beings, including humans, must contribute to that order.

But unlike celestial bodies that move in fixed patterns, humans have free will i.e., the ability to either align with this order or disrupt it. If they fail to maintain balance, if disorder spreads unchecked, it begins to affect Earth's stability. This, in turn, affects the solar system, ultimately steering it toward its predetermined path of destruction.

In essence, the Observable Realm is non-deterministic only as long as it maintains order. If it ceases to do so, it merely falls in line with the Dark Realm's natural course. This suggests that our role, whether through individual choices, societal structures, or technological advancements is crucial in influencing the longevity of the universe's balance.

The Purpose of Life: Beyond Survival

I don't think the sole purpose of life is just to survive. I believe that life has a dual purpose: to survive and to evolve while maintaining the order of the system associated with a civilization. This order is crucial for the sustained existence of the universe.

So, how does one evolve? I believe two key steps are necessary:

  • Understanding the Nature- This includes understanding the environment in which we exist.
  • Reducing Thought Clutter and Asking "Why" - Instead of being controlled by numerous scattered thoughts, one should reduce their thoughts and focus on questioning the fundamental reasons behind natural phenomena.

Why Understanding Nature is Essential for Evolution

If humans wish to evolve, they must first understand the environment they exist in, including the laws of nature, and even the celestial interactions.

To explain why this is crucial, Consider an iron rod, no matter how small a fragment you take from an iron rod, it retains the properties of iron because its atomic structure remains the same. Similarly, everything in the universe, including us, is made of the same fundamental particles and governed by the same physical laws. This means that by studying any part of nature, we gain insights into the fundamental principles that shape the universe.

As we seek to understand nature, we naturally begin to ask deeper "why" questions - questions that drive us toward higher knowledge and evolution.

The Role of Thoughts in Evolution

Our thoughts shape our perception and actions, but they can also become obstacles to evolution.

A newborn child has very few thoughts perhaps only those imprinted genetically. As they grow, they absorb thoughts from their surroundings: society, family, and experiences. Eventually, these accumulated thoughts begin to control their decisions and actions.

However, if these thoughts are unstructured, they can lead to disorder, and as we discussed earlier, disorder leads to destruction. Just as celestial bodies must follow an order to sustain their existence, humans must align their thoughts in a structured way to survive and evolve.

By reducing unnecessary thoughts and focusing on fundamental questions, humans can break free from societal conditioning and begin to understand the universe at a deeper level.

Evolution as Humanity's Responsibility

If evolution is necessary for maintaining order in the universe, then it is not just a personal journey but it is a responsibility.

Each generation must fulfill a specific task that contributes to the progress of the next. Let's call this the "Evolutionary Task." This task may be singular or a collection of interconnected goals. Over multiple generations, as these tasks are completed, humanity moves to the next level of evolution.

If humans fail to evolve and instead embrace disorder, they will disrupt the order of Earth, which in turn will disrupt the solar system, ultimately aligning the Observable Realm with the deterministic path of the Dark Realm, leading to destruction.

Therefore, evolution is not just about advancing technology or intelligence. It is about maintaining order, aligning with the universe's deeper flow, and ensuring the continuity of existence itself.

The Brain as a Decoder of Universal Information

This idea may seem fictional, but I believe the human brain has the potential to decode information from the universe.

The universe is filled with information, possibly in the form of energy waves or signals sent by higher intelligences. When a person reduces mental clutter, their mind may resonate with these information waves, altering their way of thinking and even generating new thoughts.

This means that evolution is not just about physical advancement but also about reaching a state where the mind can tap into deeper layers of universal knowledge.

Earth was not created by humans , we are merely inhabitants of this planet. It existed long before us and will eventually be destroyed, just like everything else in the universe. However, the window for intelligent life to evolve on a habitable planet is rare. The formation of a life-sustaining planet like Earth does not happen often, making our presence here a significant opportunity.

Because Earth will not last forever, our purpose is not just to live and die here but to evolve and eventually move beyond this planet. Before Earth reaches its inevitable destruction, humanity must progress to a level where we can migrate to other celestial bodies or even different dimensions of existence. This is not just a survival strategy but it is a continuation of the universal order.

But what happens after we leave?

It is possible that Earth will continue to exist for a while after humanity's evolution is complete. If so, another intelligent species may arise, just as we did, and go through the same cycle of survival, evolution, and departure.

In fact, if we look at history from a broader perspective, there is a possibility that before humans, a highly intelligent species once existed on Earth. They may have completed their evolutionary journey, moved on to a higher plane of existence, and left behind a space for us to emerge.

This cycle of civilizations may be a universal phenomenon, where planets serve as temporary platforms for intelligent life to develop before they outgrow their physical limitations and transition to the next stage of existence.

Conclusion

If we view life as merely a struggle for survival, we risk missing the greater purpose of our existence. I believe that life is not just about surviving but it is about evolving, ensuring that the system associated with our civilization remains in order, and ultimately contributing to the continuity of the universe itself.

The observable realm, in which we exist, lags behind absolute time, allowing us the opportunity to alter our future. This lag grants us the ability to make choices, to shape our civilization's path, and to steer away from the natural tendency toward entropy and destruction. If we fail to maintain order then both within ourselves and in the systems we are part of, we risk being pulled into the predetermined path of the Dark Realm, where all things move inevitably toward their end.

Evolution is not an individual process but a generational task. Each civilization must pass on knowledge, enabling the next generation to build upon it. If we remain stagnant, lost in chaotic thoughts and distractions, we risk being just another temporary species that rises and falls, leaving no meaningful contribution to the larger cosmic process.

But if we consciously work toward understanding nature, aligning our thoughts, and asking "why" about the world around us, we can break free from the cycle of destruction. Our brains may even have the potential to decode signals embedded in the universe by higher civilizations that have already evolved beyond our level.

Earth is not our final destination but it is a stepping stone. If we evolve, we will move beyond it, making way for the next intelligent species to begin their journey. This cycle of existence and evolution may be the true rhythm of the universe.
