The Order of the Universe: A Hypothetical Exploration

In this post, I want to share my thoughts about the order of the universe. The ideas shared in this post build upon the concepts discussed in the previous one. If you haven't read it yet, you can check it out here.

I think, for anything to exist, whether it's a thing, a system, or the universe itself then it must maintain some form of order. The universe, however, doesn't have intelligence to keep everything in order. So, how does it happen?

Intelligent life forms: What is the purpose?

In the beginning, I think the universe was likely chaotic. Matter and energy would have been in a random, unpredictable state, with no clear patterns. But from what I've been considering, randomness might not be as chaotic as we often think. Over time, I believe that randomness can lead to order, especially through small interactions and subtle changes that build up over time.

Now, I wonder if life itself could have emerged from this random order. Life isn't something that just "happens"; rather, it seems to be the result of certain conditions aligning in just the right way. Over time, these alignments could have created something new in the universe called intelligent life. I'm just exploring this idea, but it seems possible that intelligence might have emerged as part of this random process, eventually creating the complex, ordered systems we see today.

However, there's another aspect to consider. Energy exchange, the creation of celestial bodies, and gravitational interactions all happen in the universe in random ways, but that doesn't necessarily mean they will keep everything in order. These forces and processes may lead to some sort of structure or organization, but they don't guarantee long-term stability. After all, external interactions, whether in the form of new forces or cosmic events, could easily disrupt whatever order has been established. So, I think something else must also be there to maintain the ongoing balance in the universe.

At this point, I started thinking about human beings on Earth. Why are we here, and what is our purpose? I asked many people around me, and most said, "We exist because the conditions are right for life, and our goal is simply to survive." While this answer made sense on a basic level, it didn't fully satisfy me. So, I pondered this question much deeper.

After some reflection, I concluded that human beings are unique. Intelligent beings, like humans, are distinct from other life forms. They possess the ability to influence and bring about changes in ways that other living beings cannot. This capacity for change might extend beyond mere survival and may be integral to maintaining or even shaping the balance and order of the universe.

At this point, you might think, "But compared to the vast universe, humans and Earth are smaller than an atom!" Here's an analogy: Consider bacterial colonies. From a human perspective, these colonies are tiny, and they're often invisible to the naked eye. They might occupy a small area - just millimeters or centimeters in size. Despite their size, they have the ability to control us in various ways, whether by impacting our health or influencing ecosystems.

Now, if we shift perspectives to view the bacteria, the space between their colonies might feel vast to them, perhaps even like light-years away. The distances may seem very large in their tiny world. Similarly, when we look at potential intelligent life forms elsewhere in the universe, we assume they are light-years away from us. But what if, from the universe's perspective, we are not as small and insignificant as we think? Just as bacterial colonies might control us, these intelligent beings in the universe could have a role in influencing or guiding the universe. They could be a key part of the cosmic process, maintaining balance and contributing to the universe's existence.

So, I think that intelligent beings are not just a result of evolution or chance, but that they play a crucial role in the universe's order acting like "organizers" or "maintainers" within the vast cosmic system. They might even serve a greater purpose that we can only begin to understand, helping the universe evolve in ways that sustain life, energy, and order across time.

Through a Futuristic Thought: Aliens

While thinking about the purpose of life, I started wondering about aliens. Where are they? Are they far away or closer than we think? I like to call all intelligent beings aliens, so in this sense, humans are also aliens.

As I mentioned before, the purpose of these beings is not just to survive - there is something bigger happening.

To understand this better, I came up with a thought experiment. It might sound like fiction, but I want to share it here.

I have categorized alien civilizations into five levels. These levels represent stages of evolution that are distinct from one another, existing in different dimensions. Aliens at each level must undergo various stages of evolution. Once an alien civilization completes these evolutionary stages, they are promoted to the next level. Evolution, in this context, is physical, intellectual, and technological.

Here's the structure of the evolutionary stages:
Level 1: Four stages of evolution. These aliens are tasked with evolving and maintaining order on their home planet.
Level 2: Three stages of evolution. As first-level aliens evolve through their stages, they reach a point where they are promoted to the second level. At this stage, their responsibilities expand beyond their planet. They are tasked with maintaining order in entire planetary systems.
Level 3: Two stages of evolution. These aliens now manage and maintain the order of entire galaxies.
Level 4: One stage of evolution. These aliens are responsible for keeping galaxy clusters in order.
Level 5: Zero stages of evolution. They oversee the proper energy conversion and balance, ensuring the cosmos runs smoothly.

Just as animals on Earth instinctively follow natural laws such as creating territories, forming hierarchical structures, and migrating when needed, there are universal laws that the aliens, too, must follow to maintain order. These are not taught to them; rather, it is an inherent part of existence. Similarily, first-level aliens, starting from zero, progress through four stages of evolution. Upon completing these stages, they come to realize their ability and responsibility to manage not only their own planet but also the nearby planets within their reach, moving forward in accordance with these universal laws.

Humans, as we stand today, belong to the first level of alien civilization, currently at the fourth stage of evolution. This stage involves developing the capacity to manage and maintain order not only on our planet but also the planets in our solar system.

Equilibrium: Key to the laws

Natural laws emerge from the equilibrium or balance found in nature. Nature maintains order through localized equilibriums within different systems, and the creatures within these systems follow specific patterns to sustain that balance. These localized interactions contribute to the broader laws that govern nature as a whole.

Similarly, the universe, being vast, consists of countless regions with their own equilibrium. For example, our solar system maintains its own balance, and as part of this system, humans may also follow certain natural principles that contribute to its stability.

Final Thoughts

The universe is a vast and complex system, but it operates in an orderly way because of certain key principles. Energy conversion, the formation of celestial bodies, and gravitational forces all work together to maintain balance. These natural processes are guided by laws derived from equilibrium, which exists in every part of the universe. Each system, like our solar system, has its own local balance, and all these local balances combine to create universal laws that keep the universe stable.

In this grand design, i think intelligent beings - aliens, play an important role. Just as animals on Earth instinctively follow the laws of nature to survive and maintain their environment, aliens evolve through stages and take on greater responsibilities. Starting from managing their own planet, they grow in their abilities to eventually help maintain the balance of planetary systems, galaxies, and beyond. This evolution is not just about survival but about contributing to the overall order of the universe.

Together, the processes of energy exchange, celestial body formation, gravitational interactions, and the efforts of intelligent beings create a system that keeps the universe in order. Each element, no matter how small, has a purpose in this vast and interconnected cosmos.

As I have mentioned, these are a product of my reflections and imaginative exploration that could inspire new ways of thinking about the universe and our role in it.
